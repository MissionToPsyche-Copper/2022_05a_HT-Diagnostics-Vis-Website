Hall Thruster Diagnostics

Testing the Propulsion to Psyche

Overview

When the Psyche Mission launches in 2022, it will be the first time humankind will be visiting an entirely metal asteroid. But how will we get there? The Psyche Mission is equipped with electronic Hall Thrusters which will be used as the main propulsion system once it has left our atmosphere! Since there will be no chance to test the thrusters in space, different diagnostic tests have been devised on Earth to analyze how the thrusters will perform in space. The thrusters exhaust a plume of plasma and ions in order to propel the spacecraft. The ions within this plume are hotter than 10,000Â°F and are travelling faster than 20,000 MPH. Therefore, it is important to understand and measure how this plume of ions is being controlled. There are two important diagnostic tests for the thrusters to pass, the Faraday Probe Diagnostics and the Retarding Potential Analyzer Diagnostics.

Faraday Probes

A faraday probe is a small cylindrical shaped object that can be used to determine the local ion charge of electric propulsion plumes. Retarding Potential Analyzer

It is important to know how the plume of ions is shaped, because the electronic thrusters rely on the electrical energy released by Ions. The energy measured within this test shows how efficient the thruster will be when it is in use.
