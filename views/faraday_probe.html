Faraday Probe

The Faraday Probe is a small device used to measure the ion current density distribution, or the number of ions that exist at a location, of the Hall thrusters. The probe consists of a metal cylinder, or "guard," that houses a flat metal disk, or "collector."

How It Works

The collector and guard are biased negatively and swept in an arc through the plume. The collector current is measured as a function of position (higher current = more ions)

What It's For

Faraday Probes are used to assess the density of the flow of ions in the plume generated by the Hall Effect Thrusters. The information gathered by these probes are especially useful in spacecraft electric propulsion applications, such as:

  • Ground predictions and flight measurements of local plume properties to determine plasma-spacecraft interactions
  • Characterization of global plume properties to assess the thruster plasma discharge and loss mechanisms
  • Validation data for modeling and simulation
  • Data for thruster acceptance tests

The diagnostics produced by the probes can also be useful in applications outside of spacecraft propulsion such as: space plasma experiments, dense plasma focus experiments, and laser-produced plasma.

  • Space plasma experiments
  • Dense plasma focus experiments
  • Laser-produced plasma
