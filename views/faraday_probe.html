What it measures: The ion current density distribution (i.e., how many ions there are at a given location)

What it is: A flat metal disk (“collector”) housed inside a metal cylinder (“guard”)

How it works: The collector and guard are biased negatively and swept in an arc through the plume. The collector current is measured as a function of position (higher current = more ions)
