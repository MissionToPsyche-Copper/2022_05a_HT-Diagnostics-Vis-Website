Retarding Potential Analyzers

At the lowest level, the Retarding Probe Analyzer (RPA) is a series of mesh grids inside of a metal housing. Its main function is to measure the energy of the ions in the thruster plume. The goal is to create a distribution of the energy of the ions in the plume. Each of the four meshes, the floating grid, electron repulsion grid, ion retarding grid, and electron suppression grid, each have a specific purpose in creating this distribution.

How it works

  • Plasma (ions + electrons) enters the front of the RPA through the floating grid
  • Electrons are stopped by the electron repulsion grid (which is negatively biased) while the ions continue [4]
  • Since the ion retarding grid is biased positively, ions with high enough energy pass through the grid and are measured by the collector while those with lower energy are stopped [4]
  • By varying the bias on the grid, we can map out how many ions have a certain amount energy (i.e., the energy distribution) [2]
  • In other words, the RPA is a high-pass filter for ions in order to gauge their energy level
